## 3. Brewing Guide

### Espresso

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grind Size | Fine (table salt) |
| Dose | 18g coffee in |
| Yield | 36g espresso out (1:2 ratio) |
| Water Temp | 93°C |
| Brew Time | 25–30 seconds |
| Pressure | 9 bars |

**Steps:**
1. Preheat your portafilter and cup.
2. Grind 18g of coffee to a fine, table-salt consistency.
3. Distribute and tamp evenly with firm, level pressure.
4. Lock in the portafilter and start your shot immediately.
5. Aim for 36g of espresso in 25–30 seconds.
6. Adjust grind finer if it runs too fast, coarser if it's too slow.

**Stoxy tip:** "This bean pulls a cracking shot — chocolatey and bright. Perfect as a flat white base."

### V60 Pour Over

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grind Size | Medium-Fine (sand) |
| Ratio | 1:16 (e.g. 15g coffee → 240g water) |
| Water Temp | 93–96°C |
| Brew Time | 3–4 minutes total |

**Steps:**
1. Rinse the V60 filter with hot water, then discard.
2. Add ground coffee and shake level.
3. Pour 30–45g of water for a 30–45 second bloom. Stir gently.
4. Continue pouring in slow, circular spirals in 3–4 stages.
5. Total brew time should be 3–4 minutes.

**Stoxy tip:** "Brilliant for tasting the citrus brightness in this Honduras. Take your time with the pour."

---

### AeroPress (Inverted Method)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grind Size | Medium-Fine (sand) |
| Ratio | 1:12 (e.g. 15g coffee → 180g water) |
| Water Temp | 85–90°C |
| Brew Time | 2–3 minutes |

**Steps:**
1. Flip the AeroPress upside down (inverted position), plunger at bottom.
2. Add ground coffee to the chamber.
3. Pour water to the top (about 180g for 15g coffee).
4. Stir 3–4 times, then place the filter cap with a rinsed filter on top.
5. Steep for 1.5–2 minutes.
6. Carefully flip onto your mug and press slowly over 30 seconds.

**Stoxy tip:** "Lower water temp keeps the brightness without any bitterness. Great travel brew too."

---

### French Press

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grind Size | Coarse (rock salt) |
| Ratio | 1:15 (e.g. 30g coffee → 450g water) |
| Water Temp | 93°C |
| Brew Time | 4 minutes steep |

**Steps:**
1. Add coarsely ground coffee to the French press.
2. Pour all water at once (93°C) and stir to saturate all grounds.
3. Place the lid on (plunger up) and steep for 4 minutes.
4. Press the plunger down slowly and steadily.
5. Pour immediately — don't leave it sitting or it'll over-extract.

**Stoxy tip:** "Coarse grind is key here. Too fine and you'll get a muddy, bitter cup."

---

### Moka Pot

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grind Size | Fine-Medium (fine sand) |
| Fill | Level basket — don't tamp |
| Heat | Medium heat |
| Brew Time | ~5 minutes |

**Steps:**
1. Fill the bottom chamber with pre-boiled water to the safety valve line.
2. Fill the filter basket level with ground coffee — do not tamp.
3. Screw together tightly and place on medium heat.
4. Keep the lid open to watch the flow.
5. Remove from heat when you hear a gurgling/hissing sound.

**Stoxy tip:** "Starting with hot water stops the bottom chamber from scorching the coffee on the way up."

---

### Cold Brew

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Grind Size | Extra Coarse (peppercorns) |
| Ratio | 1:8 (e.g. 50g coffee → 400g water) |
| Temperature | Room temp or fridge |
| Brew Time | 12–24 hours |

**Steps:**
1. Combine coarsely ground coffee with cold or room temperature water.
2. Stir to ensure all grounds are saturated.
3. Cover and leave at room temperature for 12 hours, or in the fridge for up to 24 hours.
4. Strain through a fine mesh sieve or filter paper.
5. Store concentrate in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.
6. Dilute 1:1 with water or milk when serving.

**Stoxy tip:** "Room temp brews faster — fridge brews slower and slightly cleaner. Both are class."

## 4. Grind Guide

| Brewing Method | Grind Size | Looks Like |
|----------------|-----------|------------|
| Espresso | Fine | Table salt |
| Moka Pot | Fine-Medium | Fine sand |
| Pour Over (V60) | Medium-Fine | Sand |
| AeroPress | Medium-Fine | Sand |
| Drip / Filter Machine | Medium | Sea salt |
| French Press | Coarse | Rock salt |
| Cold Brew | Extra Coarse | Peppercorns |

**Key rule:** Grind finer → stronger, slower extraction. Grind coarser → lighter, faster extraction. Always adjust based on taste — bitter means grind coarser, sour/weak means grind finer.
