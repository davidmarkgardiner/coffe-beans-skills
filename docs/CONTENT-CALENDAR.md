#### Wednesday - Brewing Tip
**Theme:** Grind Size Matters

**Draft Caption:**
```
The grind size cheat sheet ☕📏

• COARSE → French Press (5-8 min)
• MEDIUM-COARSE → Chemex (3-4 min)
• MEDIUM → Drip/Pour-over (2-3 min)
• MEDIUM-FINE → V60/Aeropress (2-2:30 min)
• FINE → Espresso (25-30 sec)

Too fine = bitter & over-extracted
Too coarse = sour & under-extracted

Goldilocks your grind. Your taste buds will thank you.

Save this for your next brew! 🔖

#GrindSize #CoffeeTips #HomeBrewing #BaristaTips #StockbridgeCoffee #Edinburgh
```

#### Wednesday - Brewing Tip
**Theme:** Water Temperature Guide

**Draft Caption:**
```
90°C. That's the number. ☕🌡️

Boiling water (100°C) scalds your coffee.
Too cool (<85°C) and you miss the full flavour.

For pour-over: 90-93°C (30 sec off boil)
For French Press: 94°C (20 sec off boil)
For AeroPress: 85-90°C

No thermometer? Just wait 30 seconds after boiling.
Your beans will reward you with sweetness instead of bitterness.

Simple. But it changes everything.

#WaterTemperature #BrewingScience #CoffeeTips #HomeBrewing #StockbridgeCoffee
```

**Image Description:** Gooseneck kettle with thermometer showing 90°C, steam rising, coffee equipment blurred in background. Clean, technical aesthetic with warm lighting.

**Draft Caption:**
```
"I don't taste chocolate or citrus. I just taste... coffee." ☕🤔

Fair. But here's the thing — tasting notes aren't literal flavours 
added to beans. They're the natural compounds that remind us 
of those foods.

Our Honduras beans have:
🍫 Chocolate notes → natural sugars caramelised during roasting
🍯 Caramel → Maillard reaction during development
🍊 Citrus → organic acids from the bean

Your brain recognises these as familiar tastes. 
With practice, you'll start noticing them too.

Start with "sweet" vs "bright" — that's usually the first 
distinction people notice.

#TastingNotes #CoffeeEducation #SensoryAnalysis #SpecialtyCoffee #StockbridgeCoffee #Edinburgh
```

#### Wednesday - Brewing Tip
**Theme:** The Importance of Fresh Grinding

**Draft Caption:**
```
Ground coffee goes stale in 15 minutes. ⚠️

Oxygen is the enemy. Once you grind, volatile aromatics 
start escaping immediately. By the time pre-ground coffee 
reaches your cupboard? Most of the magic is gone.

Solution: grind fresh, every time.

Budget grinder > expensive pre-ground

Your nose knows the difference. Trust it.

Invest in a burr grinder. Your beans deserve it.

(Sorry, Fox is passionate about this one. He's been known

#FreshGrind #CoffeeScience #HomeBrewing #BaristaTips #StockbridgeCoffee #Edinburgh
```
