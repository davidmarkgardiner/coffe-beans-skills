# Stockbridge Fox Video Scripts

## Script 1: Morning Coffee Routine
**Duration:** 15-20 seconds  
**Topic:** The fox's morning routine and love for coffee  
**Tone:** Relatable, humorous, warm  
**Setting:** Behind the coffee shop counter, morning light

---

### Script

**[Fox looking slightly dishevelled but functional, holding a cup of coffee]**

"Right, morning everyone. Here's how this works.

I wake up. I have an espresso. I have another espresso. 
Then I open the shop and pretend to be a functional adult.

By ten o'clock, I'm on my fifth cup and genuinely believe 
I could run a marathon. I won't. But I could.

Stockbridge Coffee — where the coffee's serious, 
but we definitely aren't. See you at the counter."

---

### Direction Notes
- **Opening shot:** Fox wiping down the counter, looking tired but determined
- **Middle:** Fox gesturing with coffee cup while talking
- **End:** Warm smile, pointing toward the camera/viewer
- **Background:** Coffee shop interior, steam from espresso machine visible
- **Lighting:** Soft morning light streaming through windows
- **Voice delivery:** Scottish-tinged, conversational, slightly self-deprecating humour

### Visual Elements
- Steam rising from cup
- Espresso machine in background
- Morning atmosphere (soft, golden lighting)
- Fox wearing barista apron

## Script 2: How to Use a V60
**Duration:** 20-25 seconds  
**Topic:** Simple V60 brewing tutorial  
**Tone:** Educational but light, encouraging  
**Setting:** Coffee brewing station, clean aesthetic

---

### Script

**[Fox standing at brewing station with V60 setup]**

"Right, you want to know how to use one of these? 
It's easier than pronouncing 'Vee-Sixty' correctly.

Fifteen grams of coffee. Medium-fine grind. 
Rinse the filter — hot water, tip it out.

Thirty grams of water to bloom. Wait thirty seconds. 
Smell that? That's the good stuff waking up.

Then pour slow circles to two-fifty total. 
Two and a half minutes. Done.

Clean cup. Bright flavours. You'll look like 
you know what you're doing. That's the main thing."

---

### Direction Notes
- **Opening shot:** Fox holding V60 dripper, looking at camera
- **Cutaways:** Close-ups of each step (grind, rinse, bloom, pour)
- **End shot:** Fox holding finished cup, satisfied expression
- **Pacing:** Steady, not rushed — match the calm nature of pour-over
- **Voice delivery:** Clear, instructional but not preachy, encouraging

### Visual Elements
- V60 dripper and carafe
- Gooseneck kettle
- Scale visible
- Freshly ground coffee
- Timer (showing bloom time)
- Steam and bubbles during bloom phase

### Pro Tips to Overlay (Optional)
- "15g coffee : 250ml water"
- "Bloom = 30g water, 30 seconds"
- "Total brew: 2:30"

## Script 3: Edinburgh Weather + Coffee
**Duration:** 15-20 seconds  
**Topic:** Classic Scottish weather observation  
**Tone:** Observational humour, relatable, warm  
**Setting:** Looking out window at rain, cosy interior

---

### Script

**[Fox looking out rain-streaked window, holding coffee cup]**

"You see this? This is why we drink coffee.

It's been raining since Tuesday. It's going to rain 
until next Tuesday. And the Tuesday after that.

But here's the thing — nowhere's cosier than 
a coffee shop when it's pelting down outside.

We've got beans. We've got warmth. And I've got 
opinions about the weather. Come on in. The kettle's always on."

---

### Direction Notes
- **Opening shot:** Fox at window, rain visible outside, contemplative expression
- **Middle:** Turn to camera, gesturing with cup
- **End:** Warm inviting gesture toward the shop/camera
- **Lighting:** Contrast between grey exterior and warm interior lighting
- **Voice delivery:** Matter-of-fact about weather, warm and inviting at the end

### Visual Elements
- Rain on windows (classic Edinburgh weather)
- Grey sky visible through window
- Warm interior lighting
- Steam from coffee cup
- Cosy coffee shop background
- Possible: passerby with umbrella outside

### Optional Scottish Touches
- "Pelting down" — Scottish phrase for heavy rain
- Relatable Edinburgh weather humour
- Sense of community and shelter from the storm
