## What Is SHG Coffee?

SHG stands for **Strictly High Grown** — and yes, the altitude matters. Coffee cherries grown above 1,200 metres mature more slowly due to cooler temperatures. This extended development period allows complex sugars and organic acids to build within the bean, creating the nuanced flavour profile that specialty coffee enthusiasts seek.

The result? Dense, hard beans that roast beautifully and brew into cups with remarkable clarity and depth.

## The Marcala Region: A Coffee Heritage

Coffee farming in Marcala stretches back over 200 years. The region sits within the Opalaca mountain range, where volcanic soil provides rich minerals and excellent drainage. Indigenous Lenca communities have cultivated coffee here for generations, developing techniques that respect both the land and the crop.

What makes Marcala special:

- **Altitude:** 1,200-1,600m elevation creates ideal growing conditions
- **Climate:** Cool nights and warm days provide temperature variation crucial for flavour development
- **Soil:** Volcanic earth rich in nutrients and organic matter
- **Harvest:** Careful hand-picking ensures only ripe cherries are selected
- **Processing:** Washed processing method produces clean, bright profiles

## From Cherry to Cup: The Journey

The journey from Honduran mountainside to Edinburgh coffee cup involves dozens of careful steps. After selective hand-picking, cherries undergo processing within hours to preserve freshness. The washed (wet) process used for our Monte Cristo Mountain beans involves pulping, fermentation, washing, and sun-drying on raised African beds.

This method highlights the bean's natural characteristics — the chocolate and caramel sweetness comes through clearly, while the citrus brightness provides a clean, refreshing finish that lingers pleasantly.

## Tasting Notes Explained

When we describe our Honduras beans as having notes of **chocolate, caramel, and citrus brightness**, we're not adding flavourings. These are natural compounds developed through the bean's genetics, growing environment, and careful roasting.

- **Chocolate** — The natural sugars in the bean caramelise during roasting, creating compounds that remind us of cocoa
- **Caramel** — The Maillard reaction during the roasting development phase produces sweet, buttery notes
- **Citrus brightness** — Organic acids (particularly citric and malic) preserved from the high-altitude growing conditions

Together, these elements create a balanced cup that's rich without being heavy, sweet without being cloying, and bright without being acidic.

## Why Single Origin Matters

In an age of coffee blends designed for consistency, single-origin coffees offer something different: transparency. When you buy our Honduras SHG Monte Cristo Mountain beans, you're buying coffee from a specific place, harvested at a specific time, processed by specific people.

This traceability matters for several reasons:

**For flavour:** Single origins showcase the unique characteristics of their terroir — the complete natural environment including soil, topography, and climate.

**For farmers:** Traceable coffee typically commands better prices, supporting sustainable livelihoods for farming communities.

**For you:** There's something special about knowing exactly where your morning cup originated. It transforms coffee from commodity to experience.

## Brewing Recommendations

Our Honduras beans shine across multiple brewing methods, but they particularly excel in methods that highlight clarity and nuance:

### V60 Pour-Over (Our Favourite)
- 15g coffee to 250ml water
- Medium-fine grind
- 90°C water temperature
- 2:30 total brew time

The V60's paper filter produces a clean cup that lets the citrus notes sparkle while preserving the chocolate body.

### AeroPress
- 15g coffee to 220ml water
- Medium-fine grind
- 85°C water temperature
- 2:00 brew time

The AeroPress produces a fuller-bodied cup that emphasises the caramel sweetness.

### French Press
- 30g coffee to 500ml water
- Coarse grind
- 94°C water temperature
- 4:00 brew time

The immersion method brings out the chocolate notes for a rich, comforting cup.

## Freshness and Storage

Like all specialty coffee, our Honduras beans are best enjoyed within 2-4 weeks of roasting. We roast in small batches at our Edinburgh facility and ship beans within 48 hours of roasting to ensure maximum freshness.

For storage:
- Keep beans in an airtight container
- Store at room temperature (not the fridge or freezer)
- Avoid direct sunlight
- Grind just before brewing (ground coffee loses aromatics within minutes)
